Phase 1: Interior Staging

Make every room feel spacious, clean, and inviting

Declutter & Depersonalize

  • Remove all family photos, personal memorabilia, and religious items
  • Clear all countertops — kitchen, bathroom, and bedroom surfaces
  • Remove excess furniture (if rooms feel tight, remove 1-2 pieces per room)
  • Empty and organize all closets (show they're roomy — aim for 30% empty)
  • Remove fridge magnets, sticky notes, and clutter from visible surfaces
  • Rent a storage unit if needed — it's worth the $50–$100/month investment

Deep Clean Everything

  • Professional deep clean or DIY: floors, baseboards, light fixtures, vents
  • Kitchen: degrease range hood, clean oven interior, polish sink and fixtures
  • Bathrooms: remove hard water stains, recaulk tub/shower if needed, polish mirrors
  • Windows: clean inside and out — natural light is your best friend
  • Carpets: steam clean or shampoo (consider replacing if stained/worn)
  • Eliminate odors: no pets, cooking smells, or air freshener overload

Finishing Touches

  • Add fresh white towels in bathrooms (rolled, not folded)
  • Place a small vase of fresh flowers in kitchen and living room
  • Use neutral-colored throw pillows and a cozy blanket on the couch
  • Set the dining table for two (simple plates, napkins, wine glasses)
  • Replace any burned-out light bulbs (use warm white, 2700K–3000K)
  • Leave all lights ON and blinds OPEN for showings
🌿

Phase 2: Curb Appeal

First impressions happen before buyers step inside

Exterior Checklist

  • Power wash siding, skirting, driveway, and walkways
  • Paint or replace the front door (high ROI — consider a bold color like navy or charcoal)
  • Add a new welcome mat and house numbers
  • Place 2-3 potted plants flanking the entrance (geraniums, succulents work great in Lancaster heat)
  • Mow lawn (or clean gravel/desert landscaping), trim bushes, pull weeds
  • Remove trash bins, garden hoses, and clutter from view
  • Check porch/deck for loose boards, peeling paint
  • If there's a carport, make sure it's clean and free of storage
  • Add solar pathway lights for evening curb appeal (under $20)
🔧

Phase 3: Quick-Win Repairs

Small fixes with big perceived-value impact

High-Impact Repairs (Under $500 Total)

  • Fix leaky faucets (buyers notice immediately)
  • Replace damaged outlet covers and light switch plates
  • Tighten loose cabinet handles and drawer pulls
  • Touch up paint on scuffed walls (keep a quart of matching paint)
  • Fix sticking doors or drawers
  • Replace cracked window screens
  • Re-seal or replace worn weatherstripping
  • Ensure all doors lock and unlock smoothly

Photography Technique Tips

  • Shoot during the day (10am–2pm) for maximum natural light
  • Turn ON all interior lights for every shot
  • Open all blinds and curtains before shooting
  • Always shoot in LANDSCAPE (horizontal) orientation
  • Shoot from room CORNERS at chest height (not eye level — slightly lower makes rooms look bigger)
  • Use a tripod or lean against a wall for sharp, blur-free images
  • Remove yourself and pets from all reflective surfaces (mirrors, TVs, windows)
  • Close all toilet lids before bathroom photos
  • Use your phone's wide-angle lens if available (0.5x on most modern phones)
  • Take 3-4 shots of each setup and pick the best one

✅ DO

  • Show depth (photograph at angles)
  • Capture natural light streaming in
  • Highlight upgrades & features
  • Include community amenity photos
  • Edit for brightness/contrast only

❌ DON'T

  • Shoot in portrait/vertical mode
  • Include people or pets in shots
  • Photograph clutter or mess
  • Use heavy filters or HDR
  • Shoot at night or in dim rooms

💡 Pro Tip: Consider Virtual Staging

  • If any rooms are empty, use a virtual staging service ($25–$75 per photo) to digitally add furniture
  • Virtually staged photos get 3x more clicks on listing platforms
  • Always label virtually staged photos as "Virtually Staged" to comply with fair advertising
